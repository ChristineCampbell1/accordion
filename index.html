FAQ About Seattle University's Online Programs

What programs do you offer?
We offer 3 online programs: BA in Digital Technology and Cultures, BA in Organizational Leadership, Certificate in Web Development
What does it mean to be online?
All of the course work is delivered in online modules. Students usually have 2 sets of deliverables due once a week: an online discussion mid-week, and some other product due Sunday by midnight.
Are online degrees different?
No. At Seattle University, online graduates receive the same diploma as traditional undergraduates do—there is no difference whether you attended online or in-person. Our professors and instructors are SU faculty and bring the same rigor and passion to their teaching of online working professional students as they do to their in-person classes.
When do your programs start?
All of our programs are built with a cohort in mind, and start once a year in the fall. Our next start date is September 27, 2020.
When can I apply?
You can apply any time. The final deadline is 3 weeks before the start of the programs: September 3, 2020. The application is short and free.
What pre-requisites do I need?
For the BA programs, the only requirements are: 45 prior credits from an undergraduate institution, and a cumulative GPA of 2.0. If you don’t quite have that, contact us anyway. There are ways to get grades up or to petition for a waiver.
